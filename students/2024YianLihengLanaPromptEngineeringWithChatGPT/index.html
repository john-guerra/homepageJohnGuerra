Advancing AI Incidents Classification: Leveraging LLMs with Strategic Prompting

Yian Chen, Lana Do, Liheng Yi, Ricardo Baeza-Yates, John A. Guerra-Gomez
https://easychair.org/publications/preprint/hb7t

Abstract

This paper presents a method for classifying AI incidents using GPT-4 and explores various prompting strategies, including zero-shot, few-shot, chain-of-thought, and tree-of-thought. The study assesses these methods through accuracy metrics and expert reviews, employing multi-value tree data visualization for clear comparative analysis. The results indicate that tree-of-thought combined with few-shot learning notably enhances classification accuracy and aligns well with expert opinions. The findings underscore the potential of LLMs in AI incident classification and highlight the importance of integrating human oversight to ensure accuracy and depth in AI governance.

Problem Statement

AI-related incidents causing societal harm require systematic classification, but manual classification is time-consuming and subjective. We investigate GPT-4's effectiveness in automating this process using strategic prompting techniques.
